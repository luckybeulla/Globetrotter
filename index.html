Top attractions

Embark on an unforgettable journey through Rwanda's top attractions, each offering a unique glimpse into the country's captivating landscape and rich history. Traverse the mist-covered slopes of Volcanoes National Park, where encounters with endangered mountain gorillas forge unforgettable connections with nature. Dive into the ancient depths of Nyungwe Forest National Park, where lush foliage hides rare primates and vibrant birdlife amidst towering trees. Pay homage to Rwanda's past at the Kigali Genocide Memorial, a poignant tribute to resilience and reconciliation. Relax by the tranquil shores of Lake Kivu, where sandy beaches and shimmering waters offer respite amidst dramatic scenery. Embark on a thrilling safari adventure in Akagera National Park, where sprawling savannas teem with iconic African wildlife. And immerse yourself in Rwanda's vibrant artistic heritage at the Rwanda Art Museum (RAM) in Kigali, where contemporary and traditional art pieces provide insight into the country's cultural identity. Each of these top attractions promises an unforgettable experience, where the beauty of Rwanda's landscapes is matched only by the warmth of its people.

Volcanoes National Park

Volcanoes national park is a stop centre for all Rwanda gorilla safaris sheltering the highest number of mountain gorillas in the Virunga Conservation area. The strategic location of the VNP roughly 2 hours drive from Kigali international airport make it the most accessible gorilla national park in the world. Besides gorillas, Volcanoes National park is a home for golden monkeys, a variety of birds, reptiles, amphibians, and insects among other creatures which together make a complete Rwanda safari package.

Akagera National Park

Embark on a safari adventure in Akagera National Park, where sprawling savannas and shimmering lakes provide a habitat for a diverse array of wildlife. From herds of antelope grazing on the plains to prowling lions and elusive leopards, Akagera offers thrilling encounters with Africa's iconic safari animals.

Kigali Genocide Memorial

A poignant tribute to Rwanda's tragic past, the Kigali Genocide Memorial serves as a solemn reminder of the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi. Through powerful exhibits and personal testimonies, visitors gain insight into the events that unfolded and the resilience of the Rwandan people as they strive for healing and reconciliation.
